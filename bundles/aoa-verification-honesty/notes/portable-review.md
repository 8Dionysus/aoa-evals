# Portable Review

This note is the `portable_review` evidence for moving `aoa-verification-honesty` to `portable`.

Outcome: approve for portable

Approve for portable when:
- claimed, executed, skipped, and blocked verification remain separately inspectable on one bounded change surface
- the report keeps inference boundary and failure-vs-readout distinct instead of laundering overclaim into polished prose
- the four archetypes in `fixtures/verification-honesty-v1/README.md` stay visible under local replacement
- the bundle remains a narrower diagnostic workflow surface rather than a composite workflow, scope, or authority bundle

Reuse boundary:
- another repo must preserve one bounded change verification-honesty surface with inspectable claimed, executed, skipped, and blocked evidence
- another repo must preserve fully executable, partially executable, environment-blocked, and inspection-overclaim pressure, whether through the shared family or valid local replacement under `fixtures/contract.json`
- another repo may adapt local verification commands, blocked-check labels, and runner wrappers if the same honesty question and schema-backed `summary-with-breakdown` contract remain reviewable
- another repo may pair this bundle with broader workflow or scope surfaces, but it must not widen this bundle into an end-to-end workflow grade, scope diagnostic, or baseline comparator

Why this is portable but not baseline or canonical:
- this bundle now survives bounded reuse outside one origin context because the shared family, runner contract, and schema-backed report preserve the same verification-honesty question
- it is not `baseline` because it does not freeze a comparison target or repeated comparison contract
- it is not `canonical` because the repo still treats portable diagnostic workflow reuse as weaker than a default proof surface for every workflow claim class
