# Agon CCS Eval Alignment

## Role

This source note names how local Agon eval prechecks may reference
Agents-of-Abyss CCS law families without becoming center law.

It is the human-facing companion to the CCS alignment seed and generated
registry.

## Route

```text
center-law reference
-> CCS alignment seed
-> generated alignment registry
-> precheck-only validation
-> bundle-local review or owner handoff
```

## Reads

Read this surface when a future Agon eval needs to ask whether contradiction,
closure, summon, or center-law pressure is represented honestly enough for a
candidate precheck.

It may cite CCS refs as stronger-owner inputs. It may not convert those refs
into `aoa-evals` doctrine.

## Boundary

`aoa-evals` owns the alignment shape, registry derivation, and precheck wording.
Agents-of-Abyss owns CCS law, center doctrine, and any live court meaning.

The alignment result stays pre-verdict. It can expose missing evidence,
misrouted law refs, or overclaim risk, but it cannot grant closure, initiate a
summon, mutate rank, write memory, or decide an arena.

## Validation

Use the validation route in the part README, part `VALIDATION.md`, and parent
`parts/AGENTS.md` child validation block:

- `mechanics/agon/parts/ccs-alignment/README.md`
- `mechanics/agon/parts/ccs-alignment/VALIDATION.md`
- `mechanics/agon/parts/AGENTS.md`

The generated registry must remain weaker than the seed, the validator, and the
stronger owner refs it cites.
