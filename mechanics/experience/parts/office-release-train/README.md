# Experience / Office Release Train Part

## Role

This part owns the support route for Experience office and release-train
verdict proof.

It keeps office scope, multi-office, service-mesh regression, governed release,
handoff, installation, boundary guard, replay audit, rollback, and train
release verdict support together.

## Source Surfaces

- `mechanics/experience/parts/office-release-train/docs/`
- `mechanics/experience/parts/office-release-train/examples/`
- `mechanics/experience/parts/office-release-train/schemas/`
- `mechanics/experience/parts/office-release-train/tests/test_experience_wave5_seed_contracts.py`
- `mechanics/experience/parts/certification-gate/schemas/rollback_drill_verdict_v1.json`
- `mechanics/experience/parts/office-release-train/docs/REPLAY_AUDIT_VERDICTS.md`
- `mechanics/experience/parts/office-release-train/docs/SERVICE_MESH_REGRESSION_VERDICTS.md`

## Inputs

- office contour and scope-fidelity packets;
- multi-office release train packets;
- service-mesh regression verdict packets;
- governed release, handoff, installation, replay, rollback, and train release
  verdict packets;
- operator go/no-go refs and rollback evidence.

## Outputs

- bounded office and release-train proof readings;
- schema/example contracts for office and release train verdicts;
- owner handoff route when evidence requires office, release, deployment,
  runtime, or operator authority.

## Stronger Owner Split

`Agents-of-Abyss` owns Experience office and release-train law. `aoa-agents`
owns actor, assistant, office, role, and handoff posture. `aoa-playbooks` owns
office, release, replay, rollback, and service choreography. `abyss-stack`
owns live runtime activation, service behavior, and deployment execution.
Owner repositories own operator approval and release adoption.
`release-support` owns release-support publication posture inside
`aoa-evals`.

`aoa-evals` owns only bounded office and release-train proof readings,
schema/example contracts, and bundle-local interpretation.

## Stop-Lines

This part must claim no office installation, assistant operational authority,
release approval, deployment approval, rollout promotion, live runtime
activation, office behavior, actor/role truth, operator approval, deployment
execution, or release-support publication.
